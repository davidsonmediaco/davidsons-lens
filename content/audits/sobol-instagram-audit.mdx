**Verdict in one line:** A mature, established brand account (~2,000 posts, real audience),
but its follower count is **underweight for a 100+ location national franchise**, and the
account currently functions more as a corporate bulletin board than a growth/conversion engine.

## 2. What's Working

- **Inherently "Instagrammable" product.** Açaí bowls are one of the most visually native
  categories on the platform (#acaibowl ≈ 1.8M posts). The raw material for great content
  exists in every store.
- **Clear, confident positioning.** "Where Fresh Meets Flavor" is short, ownable, and
  on-brand. The bio leads with product and reinforces scale ("100+ locations").
- **Conversion infrastructure exists.** App download + loyalty/rewards + e-gift cards are
  referenced — most local F&B accounts have none of this. The funnel pieces are in place.
- **Disciplined following count (558).** A healthy follower-to-following ratio (~38:1)
  signals an organic, non-spammy account.

## 3. The Core Problems

### a) Scale mismatch — followers are too low for the footprint
21K followers across 100+ locations is roughly **~200 followers per store**. A single
well-run location often holds 2–5K local followers. The corporate handle is not capturing
the audience the physical footprint already generates. Likely cause: location accounts
(e.g. @sobol_statenisland, @sobol.moorestown, @sobol_usf, @sobol_rochester,
@sobol_greatneck) operate as silos with no funnel back to the flagship.

### b) Fragmented account ecosystem
Dozens of franchisee-run handles use inconsistent naming (`sobol_`, `sobol.`, `_inc`,
`.inc`). This dilutes brand equity, confuses search, and prevents engagement from
compounding centrally. A same-name collision (a windows/doors "Sobol Inc." at @sobol.inc)
further muddies discovery.

### c) Engagement is likely below potential
2026 benchmarks put average IG engagement around 0.5–0.7%, with food & beverage typically
able to outperform at 1–3% for accounts of this size. For a product this visual, anything
under ~1.5% leaves reach on the table. *(Live post engagement could not be measured —
verify with native Insights.)*

### d) Bio is descriptive, not directive
It explains what SoBoL is but appears to spread its single link across four competing goals
(Order / Find a location / Download app / Franchise), which dilutes click-through.

## 4. Prioritized Recommendations

**Quick wins (0–30 days)**
1. **Audit the link-in-bio.** Move to a branded link hub with a clear hierarchy:
   Order Now → Find a Location → Download App → Franchise. Track clicks.
2. **Standardize the bio CTA** around one primary action (app download drives repeat
   revenue + first-party data).
3. **Reels-first content shift.** Reels are the most reliable organic reach lever in 2026.
   Target 3–4/week: bowl builds, "satisfying" pours, behind-the-counter, customer reactions.

**Structural (30–90 days)**
4. **Network playbook for franchisee accounts** — naming convention, branded template kit,
   and a content-sharing system so location posts feed the flagship (and vice versa). The
   single biggest unlock for follower growth.
5. **UGC engine.** Customers already photograph their bowls. Add an on-receipt/in-store
   handle + hashtag prompt and a monthly repost/feature program.
6. **Reclaim search.** Address the @sobol.inc name collision via consistent handle/keyword
   optimization and a verified-account push.

**Measurement**
7. **Establish a baseline** with native Insights: engagement rate, Reels vs. static reach,
   profile-visit-to-website-click rate, follower growth/week. Re-audit in 90 days.

## 5. Methodology & Caveats

- Instagram and mysobol.com both return **403 to automated requests** — the live grid,
  Stories/Highlights, posting cadence, and post-level engagement could not be pulled directly.
- Follower/following/post counts come from **publicly indexed profile metadata**, accurate
  to the last crawl, not real-time.
- Engagement commentary is **benchmarked, not measured.** Converting this into a hard-numbers
  audit requires either (a) screen captures of the live profile + ~12 recent posts, or
  (b) access to native Insights / a paid analytics tool.
