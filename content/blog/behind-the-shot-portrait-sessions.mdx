Most people show up to a portrait session nervous. That's normal. What you probably don't know is that I spend the first ten minutes doing almost nothing — no camera in your face, no "stand here and smile." We just talk.

## Why the First Ten Minutes Matter

The way someone holds themselves when they're relaxed versus when they're performing for a camera are two completely different things. My job isn't to capture the performance. It's to find the moment when you forget I'm there.

That doesn't happen in the first frame. It barely happens in the first ten frames. So we burn through some bad ones on purpose — loosen up, find your rhythm, stop worrying about whether your chin is in the right place.

By the time we're thirty minutes in, something shifts. You stop thinking about the camera. That's when the real work starts.

## What I'm Actually Looking For

When I'm behind the lens, I'm not waiting for you to pose. I'm watching for:

- The slight exhale after a big laugh
- The moment you look away and then back
- The half-second before you say something

Those in-between moments are almost always the best ones. They're not manufactured. They can't be directed. You just have to be ready for them.

## The Gear Doesn't Matter As Much As You Think

People ask me about camera bodies and lenses constantly. And sure, the right glass for the right light matters. But I've seen stunning portraits shot on gear half as expensive as mine, and I've seen technically perfect shots that are completely lifeless.

What matters is the relationship between the photographer and the subject. Everything else is secondary.

## What to Actually Do Before Your Session

If you've booked a session with me, here's what I want you to do beforehand:

1. **Don't over-plan your outfit.** Wear something you feel good in — not something you think photographs well. You can feel discomfort through a lens.
2. **Tell me one thing you hate about being photographed.** Seriously. It helps me know what to avoid and what to work around.
3. **Show up with ten extra minutes.** Rushing to a shoot puts you in a defensive mental state before we've even started.

That's it. I'll handle the rest.
